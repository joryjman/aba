KimiJungUn

v.

Ministry of Justice

Introduction

This is a civil action brought by the Plaintiff, KimiJungUn, against the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) for an unlawful arrest and charge of Assault lacking factual and evidentiary basis. The Ministry of Justice arrested and penalized the Plaintiff despite the absence of any demonstrable harm, damage, or qualifying assault under CityRP standards, constituting government overreach and a violation of constitutional protections.

Parties

Plaintiff Kimijungun - A citizen of Azalea Isles
Defendant Ministry of Justice - The arresting agency
Factual Allegations

On January 19, 2026, the Ministry of Justice filed Indictment #208 (Exhibit A) against the Plaintiff, charging him with Assault x1.
The arresting officer alleged that the Plaintiff “whacked” the officer multiple times.
As punishment, the Plaintiff was sentenced to 5 minutes jail time and fined $50.
The sole piece of evidence submitted by the Ministry of Justice was a screenshot taken from the officer’s point of view.
Said screenshot explicitly displays the Minecraft HUD showing zero hearts lost, indicating no damage was taken by the alleged victim.
The Ministry of Justice nevertheless proceeded with arrest, charge, punishment, and record entry.
Constitutional Violations

Violation of Article 1 – Fundamental Protections

Article 1, Section 10 guarantees protection against government overreach of powers not specified by law.

The Ministry of Justice exercised punitive authority absent:

Proof of harm
Proof of injury
Proof of a completed assault
Punishment was imposed solely on allegation, contradicting constitutional safeguards.

Violation of Article 1, Section 9 – Right to Self-Representation or Counsel

Article 1, Section 9 of the Constitution of Azalea Isles guarantees every citizen “the right to self-representation or counsel in a court of law.”

In this matter, the Plaintiff was arrested, charged, fined, jailed, and recorded criminally without being afforded any meaningful opportunity to contest the charge, seek counsel, or represent himself before punishment was imposed.

The Ministry of Justice executed a summary punishment based solely on an officer allegation, bypassing:

Judicial review
Evidentiary challenge
The Plaintiff’s right to defend himself
Such conduct effectively nullifies Article 1, Section 9, reducing the right to counsel or self-representation to a nullity whenever the Ministry of Justice elects to act unilaterally.

Failure of Evidence

The Ministry of Justice’s own submitted evidence disproves the charge:

The interface shows no hearts lost
The "Fight initiated" message cannot prove the Plaintiff committed assault. The screenshot was 25 seconds after the alleged assault took place (fight initiated timer is 45 seconds as shown in Exhibit B) which gives the reporting officer plenty of time to leave safe zones such as spawn.

As such, the charge is factually unsustainable.

Legal Claim

Unlawful Arrest and Punishment by the Ministry of Justice

The Plaintiff was arrested, fined, jailed, and recorded criminally without factual cause, in violation of constitutional protections.

Damages

As a direct result of the Ministry of Justice’s actions, the Plaintiff suffered:

Wrongful loss of liberty (jail time)
Financial loss ($50 fine)
Reputational harm via criminal record entry
Improper exercise of state power against a citizen
Prayer for Relief

The Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court grant the following relief:

Immediate expungement of Indictment #208 from the Plaintiff’s criminal record
Compensatory damages of $550 ($50 fine + $1 per second in jail)
Punitive damages of $10,000
Any further relief the Court deems just and proper

According to the criminal code, assault is defined by the following:

(b) Assault
(i) "Assault" shall be defined as the infliction of bodily harm or the threat of imminent harm upon another person, excluding acts committed in reasonable self-defense.
(ii) This offense shall be punishable by 5 minutes of jail time and a monetary fine of $50.
