After some consideration, the Court has decided dismiss this case before issuing a summons.

The Plaintiff purports to assert a civil claim for assault but relies almost exclusively on references to the criminal code and conclusory assertions of wrongdoing. The civil complaint does not clearly plead the elements of a recognized civil cause of action in a manner sufficient to invoke the Court’s jurisdiction. Further to this, in responding to the Court's questions, the Plaintiff expressly acknowledged the absence of corroborating evidence for key allegations, including intent, paralysis, and the extent of emotional or physical harm. Simply a screenshot showing that the Defendant started a fight does not justify the degree of "emotional harm" that the Plaintiff is alleging.

It is also beyond clear that this is an unserious case in the prayer for relief, given that there is no lawful basis behind the exorbitant amounts of $5200 for "emotional distress," $2000 for "a therapist," and $3299 for "post traumatic stress disorder."

The Court will not compel the Defendant to answer a complaint that is legally deficient on its face. It can be assumed that based on the lack of sufficient evidence or actual damages being sought here that the Plaintiff was simply hoping that the Defendant would be non-responsive to a summons and that they'd be able to get a default judgement. Let me be clear - the summons process is not a tool for curing foundational pleading defects. Frivolous cases will not be entertained by the District Court.

Therefore, this matter is hereby dismissed with prejudice.
